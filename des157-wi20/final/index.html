For most people who talk to today's technology — whether it's Amazon's Alexa, Apple Siri or the Google Assistant — the voice that talks back is female with a female persona. Due to innate biases, studies have found that we prefer our virtual assistants to be female when they are submissive while be prefer authoritarian voices to be male. To combat the existing sexism in the tech industry, I introduce Bali, a new gender-neutral option.

One of my big goals with Bali was to challenge gender stereotypes. There are more than 57 million smart-speaker users in the U.S. according to a September 2018 survey conducted by Voicebot.ai, and that number is only expected to grow. Bali acts as a more inclusive voice option for the future.

For this project, we limit the scenario of this application to a kiosk which would have Bali to give users the best experience.
